Intermediate Course 1

Indian Federalism

Federalism is a system of government where power is shared between a central authority and regional units, ensuring local governance within a unified state.

Intermediate Course 2

Judiciary System in India

The Judiciary System in India is an independent body that ensures justice through its hierarchical structure of courts, with the Supreme Court at the top.

Intermediate Course 3

Law-Making Process

The Law-Making Process is the formal procedure through which laws are proposed, discussed, debated, and enacted by legislative bodies to govern society.

Amendments to the Constitution Check Now

Amendments to the Constitution refer to formal changes or additions made to the original text to address evolving needs and challenges.

Constitutional Law

Constitutional Law deals with the interpretation and application of a country's constitution, defining the structure, powers, and responsibilities of government institutions.

Establishes a comprehensive framework for resolving civil disputes in Indian courts, ensuring fairness and efficiency in the legal process.
